About KUET Debating Society

Learn our journey, meet the executive committee, and discover the values that drive every workshop, tournament, and weekly practice session since our founding.

Club History

KUET Debating Society was founded in 2011 by a group of engineering students who believed that technical education should be paired with the ability to communicate, argue, and lead. What started as informal debate sessions in a common room grew into a registered student organization within the university within two years of founding.

By 2015, KDS had participated in its first national inter-university competition, reaching the semi-finals and returning with its first Best Speaker award. That year marked the beginning of a competitive tradition that now spans over two dozen national finals.

Today the society runs weekly practice sessions, multiple internal tournaments per semester, and a rigorous annual training camp. We have sent teams to competitions hosted by BUET, BRAC University, Dhaka University, and international circuits in the South Asian region.

Our Core Values

Every decision we make as a club is guided by four principles.

Intellectual Integrity

We argue from evidence and sound reasoning. We do not win by rhetoric alone. We train members to identify logical fallacies, cite credible sources, and engage honestly with opposing arguments.

Inclusive Participation

Every KUET student is welcome regardless of experience, department, or background. We run beginner-friendly onboarding each semester so no one feels shut out by jargon or competitive culture.

Competitive Excellence

We take competition seriously and train to win. Our prep camps, coaching sessions, and internal tournaments are designed to raise the skill ceiling for everyone who commits to improvement.

Community & Mentorship

Senior members coach juniors, alumni stay connected, and we celebrate each other's growth as much as our wins. The relationships built in KDS last well beyond graduation.

Programs & Training

What a typical semester looks like as a KDS member.

Weekly Practice Rounds

Every Thursday at 4:30 PM we run full-format debate rounds with adjudication and feedback. Open to all members. Beginners are paired with experienced partners.

Public Speaking Workshops

Monthly workshops focused on delivery, structure, and stage presence. Led by senior members and occasionally by guest trainers from the national circuit.

Internal Tournaments

Two competitive internal tournaments per semester give members real judged experience before facing external opponents. Results feed into national team selection.

Adjudication Training

Not every role is as a speaker. We train certified adjudicators who go on to judge at national and international competitions, earning their own recognition on the circuit.

National Tournament Prep Camp

An intensive pre-competition camp held before major national events. Covers motion analysis, case building, role strategy, and mock rounds against peer institutions.

Freshers Orientation Tournament

Held every semester to welcome new members. A low-stakes, mentored tournament format where first-timers get their first experience on the floor with full support from seniors.

Mission Statement

Our mission is to cultivate principled communicators and critical thinkers through inclusive debate culture, rigorous training, and respectful discourse. We aim to empower KUET students to express ideas with confidence, engage in evidence-based argument, and contribute meaningfully to public and professional life in Bangladesh and beyond.
